How is Lyme
disease transferred?

A tick finds its way onto your skin, takes a nice big bite and releases the Lyme spirochetes, along with other nasty bacteria, into your system.

The telltale sign of a tickbite with Lyme spirochetes is a bullseye rash. If you ever find one of these on your body, seek immediate medical attention.

How can dog trends predict Lyme disease in humans?

With a much higher risk of exposure to ticks than humans, dog trends have been speculated to predict endemic areas and trends within the human population. This study, which analyzed dog trends from 2011-2015 and human trends from 2004-2013, found that 35 of 48 states share an increasing trend of positive cases, a 73% accuracy rate.

Increasing Trends

Out of 48 states, 35 have human and dog infection rates that are increasing.

Decreasing Trends

Out of 48 states, 0 have human and dog infection rates that are decreasing.

Opposite Trends

Out of 48 states, 12 have human infection rates that are increasing and dog infection rates that are decreasing.

Prediction Accuracy

With a 73% accuracy rate, location specific trends of Lyme in humans can be predicted from Lyme trends in dogs.
